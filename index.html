Lake Naroch

Фото: Магомед

Main Info

Narach is a part of the Narach lake group (the others being Miastra (Belarusian: Мястра), Batoryn (Belarusian: Баторын), Blednaje (Belarusian: Бледнае). It was formed about 11 thousand years ago after the Pleistocene ice ages. It has a surface area of 79.6 km2, a wider length of 12.8 km, a maximum depth of 24.8 m, average depth of 8.9 m,[1] a volume of 710 million cubic meters. The lake is surrounded with pine forests. The Narach River flows out of it

History of lake

People settled near the lake about 10 thousand years ago. Linguists think that its name is probably from the Indo-European root *nar- often found in names of bodies of water (e.g., the Nara and Neris rivers). Archaeologists have excavated many burial mounds of the Baltic and Slavic people that lived around Narach.

Belovezhskaya Pushcha

60 km from the city of Brest, on the border with the Republic of Poland, there is an amazing natural monument, which many call one of the wonders of the world of our continent. The Belovezhskaya Pushcha, covered with legends, is the last great forest in Europe. To this day, the same pristine thickets have been preserved, which covered vast territories hundreds and thousands of years ago, 900 species of plants, including rare and endangered, grow in Belovezhskaya Pushcha.The park is famous for its ancient named oaks, whose age exceeds 500 years, 59 species of mammals and 227 species of birds are found in the National Park "Belovezhskaya Pushcha".
